Navratri

Navratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga and her nine forms, celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm across India.

The festival spans nine nights and ten days, usually falling in the months of September or October according to the Hindu lunar calendar.

During Navratri, devotees observe fasting, perform prayers, and organize special rituals to honor the divine feminine energy known as Shakti.

In Gujarat and many other states, people perform the traditional Garba and Dandiya Raas dances, creating a vibrant and joyful atmosphere.

Each day of Navratri is associated with one of the nine forms of Goddess Durga, symbolizing strength, wisdom, and protection.

Beautifully decorated temples, homes, and community halls are filled with devotional songs, lights, and cultural programs.

On the eighth and ninth days, known as Ashtami and Navami, special prayers and offerings are made to young girls, symbolizing the goddess herself.

The tenth day, Vijayadashami or Dussehra, marks the triumph of good over evil, commemorating the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana.

Navratri is not only a religious celebration but also a festival of unity, joy, and cultural expression, bringing families and communities together.
